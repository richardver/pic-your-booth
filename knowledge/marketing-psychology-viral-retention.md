# Marketing Psychology — Viral Loops & Retention

## Viral Loop Design for PYB

### The PYB Viral Loop

```
Guest experiences photobooth at event →
Photos shared on social media (tagged/branded) →
Friends see photos and ask "where was this?" →
Guest or host mentions Pic Your Booth →
New potential customer discovers PYB →
Books for their own event →
More guests experience photobooth → ...
```

### PYB Viral Assets

| Asset | Viral Mechanism | Optimization |
|---|---|---|
| **Printed photos** | Physical keepsake taken home, shown to others | PYB branding on photo strip (subtle, stylish) |
| **Digital photo strips** | Shared via WhatsApp, Instagram Stories | Include PYB watermark + link/QR to website |
| **Event highlight reels** | Posted on PYB socials, shared by hosts | Tag host, encourage reposting |
| **AI photobooth results** | Novelty drives sharing ("look what this AI did!") | Branded share page with "Book for your event" CTA |
| **DJ set clips** | Posted on DJ socials, tagged in event stories | Link back to PYB combo offering |

### K-Factor Optimization for PYB

```
K = invites per user × conversion rate

Example:
- Average event: 100 guests
- 60% use photobooth = 60 people
- 40% share on social = 24 shares
- Each share seen by ~200 followers = 4,800 impressions
- 1% click to PYB = 48 website visitors
- 5% submit form = 2.4 leads per event

If PYB does 4 events/month = ~10 organic leads/month from viral alone
```

### Amplifying the Viral Loop

1. **Make sharing effortless**: QR code at booth → instant digital photo → one-tap share
2. **Brand the output**: Every photo strip has subtle PYB branding + website
3. **Incentivize sharing**: "Share je foto en maak kans op een gratis photobooth voor je verjaardag"
4. **Create shareable moments**: AI photobooth, GIFs, boomerangs — content people WANT to share
5. **Tag and track**: Branded hashtag (#picyourbooth), track mentions, engage with shares
6. **Post-event email to host**: Provide sharable gallery link, make it easy for them to post

## Referral Program Design

### Double-Sided Referral

| Party | Reward | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| **Referrer** (past customer) | €50 korting op volgende boeking | Incentive to recommend + reason to rebook |
| **Referee** (new customer) | €50 korting op eerste boeking | Lowers the barrier, feels like a gift from friend |

### Referral Triggers (When to Ask)

| Moment | Why This Works | How to Ask |
|---|---|---|
| Post-event (day after) | Peak happiness, fresh memories | "Had je een geweldige avond? Ken je iemand die ook een feest plant?" |
| Photo gallery delivery | Re-experiencing the joy | Include referral link in gallery email |
| Google review request | Already engaged in sharing | "Deel je ervaring en geef een vriend €50 korting" |
| Seasonal (wedding/party season) | Top of mind for events | "Feestseizoen komt eraan — ken je iemand?" |

## Retention & Repeat Bookings

### Why Retention Matters for Event Businesses

Unlike SaaS, event bookings aren't monthly. But repeat and referral bookings are the cheapest leads:
- **Corporate clients**: Annual events, quarterly team events
- **Wedding couples**: Also plan birthdays, anniversary parties
- **Party organizers**: Multiple events per year

### Customer Health Signals

| Signal | Meaning | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Left 5-star Google review | Happy customer, high advocacy potential | Send referral link, add to VIP list |
| Shared photos on social | Actively promoting | Thank them, reshare, offer referral reward |
| Booked 2+ times | Repeat customer | VIP pricing, first-to-know for new services |
| No response to follow-up | Might be neutral or unsatisfied | Send satisfaction check, address any issues |

### Post-Event Sequence

| Timing | Action | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (event day) | Capture content at event | Social content for PYB + customer |
| Day 1 | Send thank-you email + "How was it?" | Open feedback loop |
| Day 3 | Send photo gallery link | Re-trigger positive emotions |
| Day 5 | Ask for Google review | Social proof for new customers |
| Day 7 | Send referral offer | Activate viral loop |
| Month 3 | "Planning your next event?" email | Repeat booking prompt |
| Month 6 | Seasonal reminder | Stay top of mind |
| Month 12 | Anniversary / "A year ago today" | Nostalgia trigger |

## Hook Model Applied to PYB Social Media

### For followers (potential customers):

| Step | PYB Implementation |
|---|---|
| **Trigger** | Instagram reel in discover, TikTok algorithm, retargeting ad |
| **Action** | Watch the video (low effort, high reward) |
| **Variable Reward** | Each video shows a different event, different energy, different reaction |
| **Investment** | Follow, save, comment → algorithm shows more PYB content |

### Building Habit of Following PYB Content

- **Post consistently** (same times, predictable schedule)
- **Variable content types** (event highlights, BTS, DJ sets, tips, reactions)
- **Engage back** (reply to every comment, DM back, create community feel)
- **Make followers feel part of the journey** (polls, questions, "help us pick the next...")

## Word-of-Mouth Amplification

### What Makes People Talk About PYB

| Trigger | PYB Moment | How to Amplify |
|---|---|---|
| **Remarkable experience** | Magic Mirror wow-factor | Make the first photo moment impressive (lighting, quality) |
| **Social currency** | "We had a photobooth AND a DJ" | Make it easy to brag (shareable content, unique props) |
| **Practical value** | "Great for any party" | Give tips content that people forward to friends planning events |
| **Emotion** | Joy, laughter, surprise | Capture genuine reactions, share them (with permission) |
| **Stories** | "The DJ played our Spotify playlist perfectly" | Encourage customers to share their story, feature on socials |

### The Exposure Loop (Automatic Virality)

Every PYB event automatically exposes the brand to 50-200+ non-customers:
- Photo strips with PYB branding taken home
- On-site equipment visible with branding
- Social shares from guests
- Host tells people "we hired Pic Your Booth"

**This is PYB's strongest viral asset** — zero cost, zero effort, built into every event.
