# Meta Ads Hook Optimizer

Rewrite your first 3 seconds to stop the scroll and dramatically improve video ad performance.

## When to Use

- Video ads have low hook rates (<25% watching past 3 seconds)
- CPMs are high (algorithm can't find engaged viewers)
- Good content but poor initial engagement
- Refreshing existing video creative
- Planning new video content

## Input Requirements

Provide:
1. **Current video description**: What happens in first 3-5 seconds
2. **Video type**: UGC, product demo, testimonial, etc.
3. **Hook rate**: Percentage watching past 3 seconds (if available)
4. **Target audience**: Who you're trying to reach
5. **Core message**: What the video ultimately communicates
6. **Constraints**: Budget, ability to reshoot, editing options

## The 3-Second Rule

Users decide in 3 seconds whether to keep watching. Your hook must:
1. **STOP** the scroll (pattern interrupt)
2. **INTRIGUE** them (curiosity gap)
3. **PROMISE** value (what they'll get by watching)

## Hook Performance Benchmarks

| Hook Rate | Performance | Action |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| <15% | Poor | Completely new hook needed |
| 15-25% | Below average | Significant improvements needed |
| 25-40% | Average | Room for optimization |
| 40-60% | Good | Minor tweaks only |
| >60% | Excellent | Study and replicate |

## Hook Formula Categories

### 1. Bold Statement/Claim
**Format**: Lead with controversial or surprising statement

**Examples**:
- "Nobody needs a [product category]... unless you want [outcome]"
- "I wasted $500 on [thing] before I found this"
- "Stop using [common thing]. Here's why."
- "This $20 product outperforms $200 ones"

**Best for**: Products challenging status quo, disruptive offers

### 2. Question Hook
**Format**: Ask question that demands an answer

**Examples**:
- "Why does everyone's [thing] look like this... but mine looks like this?"
- "Have you ever wondered why [common problem]?"
- "What would you do with an extra 2 hours every day?"
- "See this [thing]? What if I told you it was only $X?"

**Best for**: Problem-solving products, curiosity-driven content

### 3. Visual Pattern Interrupt
**Format**: Start with unexpected visual

**Examples**:
- Extreme close-up then pull back
- Before/after split screen
- Someone reacting dramatically
- Unexpected setting for the product
- Breaking something, spilling something

**Best for**: Product demos, transformation stories

### 4. Direct Address
**Format**: Speak directly to specific viewer

**Examples**:
- "[Specific group], stop scrolling"
- "This is for the [specific situation] people"
- "If you've ever [pain point], watch this"
- "POV: You just discovered [benefit]"

**Best for**: Niche targeting, specific pain points

### 5. Story Opening
**Format**: Begin mid-story for immediate intrigue

**Examples**:
- "So there I was at [unexpected place]..."
- "Three months ago, I couldn't even [thing]..."
- "My friend texted me 'you have to try this'..."
- "I wasn't going to post this, but..."

**Best for**: UGC, testimonials, brand storytelling

### 6. Demonstration Hook
**Format**: Show the result immediately

**Examples**:
- Show finished result, then "here's how"
- Side-by-side comparison (3 seconds)
- Speed demonstration (how fast it works)
- Size/scale demonstration

**Best for**: Product demos, tutorials, transformations

### 7. Sound-Based Hook (when audio on)
**Format**: Lead with attention-grabbing audio

**Examples**:
- Trending audio/sound
- Satisfying ASMR sounds
- Unexpected statement
- "Okay but hear me out..."
- Music beat drop with visual

**Best for**: Platforms where audio is common (Reels, TikTok-style)

### 8. Text Overlay Hook
**Format**: Large text dominates first frame

**Examples**:
- "WAIT" or "STOP"
- "$1,000 mistake" (then reveal)
- "POV:" format
- "Day 1 vs Day 30"
- "What [experts] don't tell you"

**Best for**: Sound-off viewing, feed content

## Common Hook Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Logo First
**Problem**: Starting with logo animation
**Fix**: Logo at end, start with hook

### Mistake 2: Slow Build-Up
**Problem**: Context before the hook
**Fix**: Hook first, context after (if they're still watching)

### Mistake 3: Generic Opening
**Problem**: "Hey guys" or "In this video"
**Fix**: Direct to value or pattern interrupt

### Mistake 4: Sound-Dependent
**Problem**: Hook only works with sound on
**Fix**: Add text overlays, visual-first hook

### Mistake 5: No Curiosity Gap
**Problem**: Everything revealed immediately
**Fix**: Tease the outcome, deliver later

### Mistake 6: Wrong Energy
**Problem**: Calm start, energy builds later
**Fix**: Match energy of middle/end at the start

## Questions to Ask

1. Can you reshoot/re-record, or only edit existing footage?
2. What moment in the video gets the best engagement?
3. What's your audience's biggest pain point?
4. Do you have multiple video versions to test?
5. What's working for competitors in your space?
