# Ads Report Generator

Create clear, professional ad performance reports that tell the story behind the numbers.

## When to Use

- Weekly or monthly client reporting
- Internal performance reviews
- Executive summaries
- Campaign wrap-up reports
- QBR (Quarterly Business Review) preparation

## Input Requirements

Provide:
1. **Report Period**: Date range
2. **Platforms**: Google, Meta, LinkedIn, etc.
3. **Metrics Data**: From ad platforms
4. **Goals/Targets**: KPIs you're measuring against
5. **Budget**: Planned vs actual spend
6. **Context**: Any relevant events, changes, or notes

## Report Structure Framework

### Essential Sections

1. **Executive Summary** - TL;DR for busy stakeholders
2. **Key Metrics Overview** - The numbers that matter
3. **Performance vs Goals** - Are we hitting targets?
4. **Platform Breakdown** - Performance by channel
5. **Campaign Analysis** - What's working, what's not
6. **Insights & Learnings** - The "why" behind the data
7. **Recommendations** - What to do next
8. **Next Period Plan** - What's coming

## Metrics Reference

### Universal Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures | Good Benchmark |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Impressions | How many saw ads | Volume indicator |
| Clicks | Engaged with ad | - |
| CTR | Click-through rate | 1-3% (varies) |
| CPC | Cost per click | Industry dependent |
| CPM | Cost per 1,000 impressions | $5-20 |
| Conversions | Completed goal action | - |
| CPA | Cost per acquisition | Target dependent |
| ROAS | Return on ad spend | 3x+ typical target |
| Conversion Rate | % who convert | 2-5% |

### Platform-Specific Metrics

**Meta Ads**:
- ThruPlay (video views)
- Hook rate (3-sec views)
- Frequency
- Relevance score

**Google Ads**:
- Quality Score
- Impression Share
- Search Lost IS (budget/rank)
- Click Share

**LinkedIn Ads**:
- Lead Gen Form fills
- Social actions
- Demographic breakdown

## Reporting Best Practices

### Do's
- Lead with insights, not just data
- Compare to benchmarks/goals
- Explain the "why" behind changes
- Use visualizations for trends
- Include specific recommendations
- Keep executive summary scannable

### Don'ts
- Don't dump raw data without context
- Don't hide bad news
- Don't use jargon without explanation
- Don't make excuses - explain and solve
- Don't forget to include next steps

## Interpreting & Communicating Results

### When Performance is Good
- Highlight specific drivers of success
- Recommend scaling opportunities
- Set new stretch goals

### When Performance is Bad
- Lead with actions being taken
- Provide context (seasonality, external factors)
- Present recovery plan with timeline

### When Performance is Mixed
- Segment the good from the bad
- Double down on what's working
- Cut or fix what's not

## Questions to Anticipate

1. "Why did [metric] change?"
2. "What's driving the increase/decrease?"
3. "How does this compare to competitors?"
4. "What should we do differently?"
5. "When will we see improvement?"
6. "Is the budget being used efficiently?"

Be prepared to answer these in your report or discussion.
