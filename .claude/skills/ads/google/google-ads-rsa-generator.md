# Google Ads RSA Generator

Write Responsive Search Ads that sound human, not like a robot wrote them.

## When to Use

- Creating new RSA campaigns
- Refreshing stale ad copy
- Improving low-performing ads
- Expanding headline/description variations

## The Problem with Robotic Ads

Most Google Ads sound like this:
- "Best [Product] - Shop Now - Free Shipping"
- "Top Rated [Service] | 24/7 Support | Get Quote"
- "Premium Quality | Affordable Prices | Buy Today"

These ads:
- Blend in with competitors
- Use worn-out phrases
- Feel impersonal and salesy
- Don't connect emotionally

## Input Requirements

Provide:
1. **Product/Service**: What you're advertising
2. **Target Audience**: Who you're trying to reach
3. **Unique Value**: What makes you different
4. **Voice/Tone**: Brand personality (professional, friendly, bold, etc.)
5. **Key Features**: Important selling points
6. **Proof Points**: Reviews, awards, numbers, testimonials

## Writing Principles

### 1. Sound Like a Person, Not a Billboard

```
ROBOTIC: "Premium Quality Products - Shop Now"
HUMAN: "Built to last, not just to sell"

ROBOTIC: "24/7 Customer Support Available"
HUMAN: "Questions at 2am? We're up too"

ROBOTIC: "Get Your Free Quote Today"
HUMAN: "See your price in 60 seconds"
```

### 2. Be Specific, Not Generic

```
GENERIC: "Fast Shipping"
SPECIFIC: "On your doorstep by Wednesday"

GENERIC: "Affordable Prices"
SPECIFIC: "Starts at $29/month"

GENERIC: "Expert Service"
SPECIFIC: "12 years and 3,000 happy clients"
```

### 3. Address the Reader Directly

```
DISTANT: "Customers save 30%"
DIRECT: "You'll save 30%"

DISTANT: "Businesses choose us for..."
DIRECT: "Your team will love..."
```

### 4. Create Curiosity, Not Just Claims

```
CLAIM: "Best prices guaranteed"
CURIOSITY: "Why do our prices seem too low?"

CLAIM: "Industry leading solution"
CURIOSITY: "The tool 500 agencies switched to"
```

## Headline Formulas That Work

### Problem-Solution
"Tired of [problem]?" → "Finally, [solution]"

### Specific Number
"[X] [people] switched this year"
"Save [X] hours every week"

### Question Hook
"Still using [old way]?"
"What if [desired outcome]?"

### Before/After
"From [bad state] to [good state]"

### Social Proof
"Why [X] [type of people] chose us"
"Join [X] companies who..."

### Contrarian
"[Industry belief] is wrong"
"Stop [common advice]"

### Outcome Focused
"[Outcome] without [sacrifice]"
"Get [result], not just [feature]"

## Words to Avoid vs Use

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| Best | [Specific claim] |
| Top | [Proof point] |
| Premium | [What makes it premium] |
| Quality | [Evidence of quality] |
| Affordable | [Actual price/savings] |
| Fast | [Specific timeframe] |
| Easy | [How it's easy] |
| Professional | [Credentials/experience] |
| Trusted | [Trust signals] |
| Solutions | [What you actually do] |

## Description Best Practices

1. **Don't repeat headlines** - Descriptions should add, not echo
2. **Include a CTA** - But make it specific: "See your options" not "Learn more"
3. **Use the full 90 characters** - More context = better qualification
4. **One idea per description** - Don't cram multiple messages
